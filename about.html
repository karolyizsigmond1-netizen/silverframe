My Story

Behind the lens

Photography found me before I found it. Growing up between Prague and Manhattan, I was drawn to the way light transforms ordinary spaces into something sacred — the way a single shaft of morning sun can turn a kitchen table into a still life painting.

After studying visual arts at Parsons and apprenticing under some of New York's most respected editorial photographers, I launched my own practice in 2014. Since then, I've had the privilege of working across 28 countries and collaborating with publications like Vogue, Harper's Bazaar, and The New York Times Magazine.

My Approach

I believe the most powerful photographs happen in the space between direction and spontaneity. My process is deeply collaborative — I work closely with every client to understand their vision, then bring my own perspective to create something neither of us could have imagined alone.

Whether it's a quiet portrait session in my Brooklyn studio or a multi-day editorial shoot in Marrakech, I bring the same intentionality: finding the moment where light, emotion, and composition converge into something timeless.

Philosophy

Every person carries a visual story. My role isn't to impose a narrative but to reveal the one that already exists — in the way someone holds their hands, in the quality of light that naturally gravitates toward them. I'm endlessly fascinated by this process of discovery.
